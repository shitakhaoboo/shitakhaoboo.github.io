MEKATILILI FELLOWSHIP

The Mekatilili fellowship offered opportunities for student engineers and tech enthusiasts to not only get exposed to real world problems but also to actively participate in solving them. There were three tracks offered up, all being site visits to the key players whose contributions made the fellowship a success. The three tracks were inclusive of team Agriculture with a site visit to Twiga Foods,the Accessibility team with a site Assocaition for the Physically disabled of Kenya and team manufacturing which had a site visit to AB3D

I was honoured to be among the team that visited the Twiga foods offices. This was an eye-opener as it was my actual first site visit with a client debrief to get a feel of the needs and expectations of the client.

After a thorough assessment of the premises and informal interviews carried out on the employees, we came up with four major areas that required addressing
  • Uncontrolled cold room access:
    This was such that the ideal ripening and storage conditions for the commodities were constantly interfered with
  • Power wastage by older cold rooms:
    The older cold rooms run under manual monitoring and reporting hence the continued interference with ideal conditions. This also led to power wastage
  • Mechanical Commodity damage during transit:
    Many commodities gotphysical damage(bruises) during transit hence lowering the quality and market value
  • Communication constraint between clientele and Twiga foods

I actively contributed and led a team as the hardware developer in prototyping and testing a viable solutions for the former two. The general idea was to have unique keys in order to access the coldrooms, what we accomplished by using rfid identifiers while using sensor data to automate operation of the older coldrooms.


KAMUKUNJI JUA KALI RECRUITMENT EXCERCISE

OBJECTIVES

  • To bring on board members of the Jua Kali sector into the KCB jirani training program under gearbox
  • To sensitize the public about the KCB tujiajiri program as well as the planned training under gearbox.
  • To carry out informal interviews with targeted recruits in order to find out:
    • The range of products produced within the kamukunji locale.
    • The various challenges encountered by the workforce of the Kamukunji Jua Kali sector in light of the line of production and marketing of products.
    • To find out about the different production processes within Kamukunji and the various machines on site that are relevant and applicable regularly so.

Introduction

The Jua Kali sector in the country is a business field which incorporates wholesomely the production and distribution chain. This, encompassing procurement, manufacturing and production as well as sales. In light of this, it is paramount to have a firm understanding of the basic operation of this sector. Over and above this, there is an even greater need for technical skills among the present influx of a young and vibrant population comprising of youth, who have ended up on the Jua kali sector, with trainings conducted informally and unregulated in way of apprenticeship. There is also the need for consistency in the various products produced with regards to quality, appearance and the general feel of the products. An even greater challenge arises in light of existing business owners who have been in the trade for quite some time, having reaped the fruits of this line of business as well as having seen the upsides and the downsides of Jua kali trade. In light of various benefits of an empowered Jua Kali industry such as the expansion of businesses, creation of more employment opportunities and introduction of better production processes, just to mention but a few, it is paramount that business owners are incorporated into the program.

Recruitment Process

Tools used:

  1. Online google forms
  2. Printed forms
  3. Infromal Interviews
The recruitment process involved the input of the leaders of the Jua Kali Association. Who over the first two days sensitised the members of the Jua Kali community about the program. The first two days ,registration was strictly done within the confines of the Jua Kali local office. This involved receiving the applicants, explaining in brief the purpose and relevance of the program, highlighting a few specific details as follows:
  • The training was a full scholarship
  • It would be for a minimum of two months
  • There would be two training sites
  • Daily training sessions
After grasping a basic understanding of the program, we signed up the applicants saving their details on the online database, collecting the forms of those who availed the required documents(photocopy of national ID and a passport photo)

Informal interviews

We held informal interviews with the Jua Kali community, with the questions being within the scope. Some of the questions asked were as follows:

  • What kind of products do you deal in?
  • What are the challenges you are having as Jua Kali industry?
  • What do you think about an opportunity to train, increasing technical skill as well as knowledge to operate various machines?

Some Challenges Facing Jua Kali Industry(Kamukunji)
  • Saturation of the market with product
  • Seasonal(yet predictable) market demand of products such as metal suitcases
  • Lack of transparency with regards to government involvement in the project hence an overhanging cloud of uncertainty with regards to betterment of jua Kali sector
